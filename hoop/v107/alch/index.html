The Alchemist's Cookbook

Every draught in the game is a pure function of its reagents. A herb carries four axes an alchemist reads — a Galenic temperament (hot/cold × dry/moist), a planet of the Seven, its metal, and a Macer degree (potency) — and the correspondence kernel turns them into effect: temperament → a combat effect, planet → a social/anima effect, metal → a mechanical lubricant, the vessel you prepare it in → its reach. This page is that grammar, the reagents that carry it, and recipes computed by actually brewing coherent herbs together. Names and lore are the storyteller's; the mechanics are here.

I · The four temperaments → combat

The dominant humour of a brew sets what it does in the arena. Faithful to Galenic pharmacology: hot & dry is caustic (a burn), hot & moist rouses (a stimulant buff), cold & moist cools (heals), cold & dry sedates (binds, slows).

II · The Seven → social & anima

A brew's dominant planet dips into a mind (anima) characteristic — the Seven are already the game's factions, so a draught's planet also names an affinity a like-aligned hearer feels harder.

III · Metals → the lubricant vertical  ·  Vessels → reach

Pressed as an oil, a herb's planet-metal tunes a mechanical player's chassis. And the vessel decides delivery: drunk (self), applied (touch), or burned to a vapor (ranged).
