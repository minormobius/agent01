1 · The world at a glance

The Tabard is a generation ship — an O'Neill cylinder — and hoop is the game you play inside it: a Caves-of-Qud-flavoured glyph world where the map is the forum. You walk an @ through a Voronoi-foam city; places anchor conversation threads; every place, message and story save is an ATProto record in somebody's own repo. The game is one wing of a four-part modelling package — the other three wings model the ship for real:

WingSurfaceWhat it models
The gamehoop.mino.mobi (this site)the infinite game — the lived-in world
The structurerind.mino.mobithe foam space-frame shell + frame solver
The thermodynamicstide.mino.mobithe radial atmosphere, fountain & sun, water/energy ledger
The ecosystembiome.mino.mobithe closed food-web box model (feeds the cafe, §6)

The playable game (§2) sits on a deterministic engine: the world is a pure function of (seed, chunk, genome), so every machine derives the same ship for ever and a permalink means something. Around the playable surfaces the site carries a ring of design-tool wings — chunkroller (roll one chunk), /nave (floor-1 design view), /rind (floor-2 design view), /forge (the industrial metabolism, eleven sub-pages), /paint (rendering playground), /econ (economies-as-ecosystems lab), and the research dossier. They are the workshop; the game is the product.

2 · The versioned surfaces

Each /vNNN/ is an independently served snapshot with its own localStorage namespace (hoop:vNNN:…), so saves never collide across versions. The discipline: one stable surface for hoopy's content/story testing, one development surface for disruptive work.

SurfaceRoleWhat it carries
v098legacy stablehoopy's earlier content/story testbed — kept frozen
v099prior devmap/fixture/combat work that became v100
v100stablethe playable nave: faction campaign (Rindwalker → Continuant → Drift), the three-deck stack, the chapter-one conclusion
v101development (current)v100 + the audited story engine (§8): scaled storyboard pacing, the external-reps gate channel, the end-goto validator fix, the both-schemas importer

Older snapshots (v090–v097) remain on disk and playable at their paths; they are history, not maintained. The engine-parts directories (v2…v8) are the kernel lineage the current engine imports from — v8/ is live load-bearing code, not a snapshot.

3 · The deck stack

The playable world is a linear stack of decks, each an unbounded Voronoi-foam floor, joined by shafts ("a chute is a port"). Descent is story-gated — the way down opens as the narrative tier climbs.

DeckNameWhat it isOpens at
—the overworldlevel zero, the green land above the city — proto at /over (§9, not yet wired in)planned
0the navefloor 1: a central commons (≥1 of every building type) ringed by six faction wards in three two-chunk lobes — Rindwalker · Continuant · Drift. Zero baddies; the peaceable city.start
1the upper rindthe arteries under the city, tagged by the Seven: Mercury hub (move·trade·learn) spoked to Mars (forge-cathedral), Venus (green deep), Jupiter (the long table). Combat arms here.narrative tier ≥ 3
2the lower rindthe deep stasis floor: Saturn cold-deep hub, Sol fusion-heart, Luna dream-archive, and the Signal Chamber — the chapter's close.narrative tier ≥ 4 + a chosen faction

Mechanics worth knowing: decks are laid at world-coordinate offsets (upper ~6000, lower ~12000 east) because they share the nave's seam lattice — co-locating would leak floors into each other through the walk-graph stitch. Each adjacent pair is joined by exactly one shaft whose ends are a teleport pair. Deck floors stream chunk-by-chunk; the down-shaft only opens once the floor above has fully streamed (building mid-stream races the incremental stitch). A dev shortcut (?dev=1) fast-forwards the whole descent for testing.

4 · The story spine — how hoopy's canon plugs in

All narrative content is authored by hoopy as a world export — currently a 720-record corpus (v101/story/world_export.json: 90 plot beats, 138 NPCs with full dialogue trees, 167 lore fragments, 126 items, 116 rumors, 83 creatures). The game engine is inference-free in the hot path: everything the player meets is either authored canon or gate-checked generated content, never a live model call.

The pipeline, source → screen

StageModuleWhat happens
1 · importstory/import.jshoopy's export → engine content_items. Handles both his schemas (flat r/n/p-tier records and the newer nested-content{} integer-tier form). servePool explodes room-bundles/wanderers.
2 · gatestory/review.js + gates.js + validate.jsthe review gate: schema/tier checks, dialogue-tree FSM validation, and reachability — every requires flag/item/rep must be producible by the pool or declared on the runtime boundary (worldExternal). Verdict PASS/BLOCK, nothing broken ships.
3 · servestory/atproto.jsthe pool is published as com.minomobi.hoop.story.content records on the morphyx service repo; the game reads it live (shared across v100/v101 — the service repo is not version-namespaced).
4 · runstory/engine.jsthe runtime store: talk/choose/take, dialogue FSM, gate state, XP. Crystallizing content fires flag.* producers.
5 · advancestory/anchors.js + progression.js + board.jstwo axes, deliberately split: revelation (auto, exploration XP) and narrative (human-paced — anchor turn-ins set flag.deck.<deck>.cleared, the level-up). The storyboard, quest log, waypoints and opening cast are all derived from his plot beats, not hand-authored.

6 · Garden · cafe · kitchen — the food economy today

A trade ↔ grow ↔ dwell triangle, all deterministic and data-injected:

PieceLoopData
Gardenplant in one of 6 plots → sleep in your dwelling to advance the day → crops mature over growthDays → harvest to pantry. A deterministic starter seed-bag makes it playable before any trade desk.garden/ark.json (built offline by build-ark.mjs)
Gacha / trade deskeach desk binds to one biome; spend 12 ◈ to pull seeds (new-crop bias 4× so collections converge); collecting every crop closes the biome.same ark, gacha.js
Kitchencook 2–4 crops into a dish scored by flavor coherence (avg pairwise cosine of compound embeddings, graded S→F); a coherent dish out-nourishes raw crops.garden/kitchen.json (offline embeddings)
Cafespend coins (arcade/quest earnings) → stamina/nourishment. A hearty plate is 2 coins — food is maintenance, not a money sink.food/biomes.json — baked from the biome wing's real viability oracle

Honest weaknesses (why §9 lists the overhaul): the loop is mechanically sound but shallow — sleep-to-grow gives no reason to return at a cadence, dishes don't do anything beyond nourishment (no buffs, no gifting, no quest hooks), the cafe is a vending machine rather than a place, nutrition is a gloss not a model (only biome viability is real), and the three pieces don't feed each other's stories — a harvested crop has no path to a cafe menu, a great dish has no audience. The economy caveats in §8 (arcade payouts vs costs) compound it.

The test lanes (what gates a change)

Everything testable headlessly is a node selftest, runnable from a clean checkout with no install: 46 suites at hoop/test/ (engine kernels, story engine, econ/nav/postal), 44 at hoop/v101/test/ (the live surface: world, spine, combat, minigames, the simulated-player integration run), plus per-wing suites (nave 36 · rind 37+34 · chunkroller · forge ~400 checks). All green as of the v101 audit. The known gap: no DOM/canvas coverage — a Playwright lane is a roadmap item (§9).

8 · The v101 audit — findings & fixes

A full sweep over the game, the story engine, the corpus, the tests and the workflows, done at the v101 handover. Four engine defects were found and fixed in v101 and the root engine (v100 and earlier stay frozen as-shipped); the rest are documented here honestly.

Fixed in v101 shipped

FindingFix
Storyboard pacing didn't scale. deriveStoryboard paced exposure beats with min_power: 1 + i — written for the 5-beat export. Against the 90-beat corpus, 85 beats demanded power tiers 6–90 (cap is 5): permanently uncompletable, the derived tier climb stalled in act I.the ramp now scales across the whole storyboard (1→5 over N beats; identical behaviour at N=5). Full exposure carries the climb to Resolution.
The root importer couldn't read the current canon. The 600/720-record corpus (nested content{}, integer tiers, world_refs) landed in June but only the vendored copies were taught the new schema — the root engine (the deployed worker's) still read the flat form: names, tiers, dialogue and refs all dropped.root story/import.js brought up to the both-schemas importer; markers, cast and milestones derive correctly again.
The review gate had no channel for faction rep. One NPC gates on min_rep: drift ≥ 1; rep is granted by the game's campaign, never by pool records — so the reachability check called it an orphan and BLOCKed the canon.gates.js accepts external.reps; WORLD_REPS declares the runtime-granted factions; worldExternal(content) also unions the derived runtime boundary (~90 journey flags) so the static manifest can't rot again.
The dialogue validator flagged vacuous gotos. hoopy's convention stamps ending choices with a filler goto + effects.end; the walker never follows it, but the static check raised 53 false ERRORs.validate.js skips the goto check on choices that end the conversation.
Two root test suites had been red since June 18 (they pinned the old 75-record export after the corpus swap).rewritten against the 600-record reality; all 90 suites green.

For hoopy — content defects in the canon 14 NPCs

With the manifest fixed, the review gate closes every reachability question on the 720-record corpus except 20 genuinely broken dialogue gotos: choices that advance to a node that doesn't exist and do not end the conversation. The runtime degrades gracefully (the conversation falls back to its start — a reset, not a crash), but each is a dead end the player can feel. The NPCs to repair:

Olo Vashti · Factor Merid Solen · Elara Vance · Tamsin Rill · Cassian Vell · Kael · Cassian · Miren Solas · Jory Bram · Vora · Cassia Morn · Wren · Zephyr · Veyra Sol

Most look like endings missing their end: true flag (gotos named leave/leave_v) or references to nodes that were renamed. The exact list is pinned by hoop/test/import.selftest.mjs — fixing one in the corpus will show up there.

9 · Roadmap — the next additive tasks

TaskWhere it stands · what it needs
1 · The overworldLevel zero: the green land above the city. A working proto lives at /over — eden.js is a pure, hydrology-ordered biosphere generator (mega-lakes → tributary streams that provably drain → Poisson-disk forest densified by wetness → bridges), already deterministic and node-tested. But it is its own model: different coordinate/tile system, no walk-graph integration, no NPCs, story, saves, or shaft down to the nave. The wiring job is the same shape as the rind decks — give it a deck slot above 0, a shaft/teleport pair to the nave, stream it through the chunk manager, and cast biome's gacha creatures onto it (the biome wing's /over already proves the sprite casting).
2 · Garden · cafe · kitchen overhaulMake the food economy fun and useful (§6 lists why it isn't yet). The design directions on the table: give dishes effects beyond nourishment (buffs, gifts NPCs react to, quest-hook ingredients); connect the triangle (grown crops reach cafe menus; the cafe becomes a place with regulars and chatter rather than a vending counter); give the garden a return cadence that isn't sleep-spam; let the kitchen's flavor-coherence scoring feed visible mastery (recipes, a repertoire); and tie it to the overworld — foraging wild ingredients above, growing cultivated ones below.
3 · Deckquest completionfinish SPINE.md's "remaining handoff" wiring so the load-bearing NPC layer drives hoopy-bot's level checks end-to-end.
4 · Combat + economy tuning passdrive the playthrough harness over the dials until the simulated deck-1 win rate lands where it should; retune prices with it.
5 · A DOM/canvas test lanePlaywright over the real page — the one thing the node suites can't see.
