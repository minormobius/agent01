# nave — content-slot manifest (floor 1)

> Generated by `node hoop/nave/gen-slots.mjs` from `manifest.js`. The distribution engine already exists
> (`v099/story/engine.js`): a room is anonymous geometry until first touch, then `interact()` deals ONE
> tier-legal, gate-passing pool item onto its stable **feature_key** and freezes it forever. This doc is the
> list of SLOTS that engine fills on the nave — **author pools against it.** Floor 1 is no-baddies, so there
> are **no creature pools** here.

## How a slot works

Every **room is a feature**. On first touch the dispatcher (`dispatch(store, player, type)`) picks unseen,
tier-legal content whose tags match the room's **role**, varied by tag, and binds it to the room's
`feature_key` (`nave:c<chunk>:r<room>` — chunk + room ordinal, deterministic per world seed).

- **role** → the dispatcher **tag** to author content for (worship, mend, trade, …).
- **faction** → a flavour tag (rindwalker / continuant / drift); commons content is faction-neutral.
- **tier** → the role's central-place rank ⇒ the band to author at: early (rev/pow 1), mid (rev/pow 2), late (rev/pow 3).
- **content types** → which pools a room of that role draws (`npc` always; `item`/`lore_fragment` per role).
- **★ anchor** → the lobe's exclusive building — the natural quest-giver / principal seat (also a `plot_beat`).

## Guaranteed every world (the role floors)

- **Commons** (the hub): **≥ 1 of every one of the 13 building types** — so each exclusive still appears here.
- **Rindwalker** lobe — over-biases `make store`; its two wards each guarantee `dwell` + `make store` + their exclusive: **worship** (high) and **mend** (mild).
- **Continuant** lobe — over-biases `serve heal`; its two wards each guarantee `dwell` + `serve heal` + their exclusive: **govern** (high) and **grow** (mild).
- **Drift** lobe — over-biases `move trade`; its two wards each guarantee `dwell` + `move trade` + their exclusive: **learn** (high) and **play** (mild).

## The six lobe anchors (quest hooks)

The exclusive building in each ward is the lobe's anchor — the obvious home for that lobe's principal /
quest-giver and a `plot_beat`. They map cleanly to the four-quest spine (unlock each lobe + descend to rind):

- **Rindwalker · worship** (high ward) — `feature_key` tag `worship` + `plot_beat`.
- **Rindwalker · mend** (mild ward) — `feature_key` tag `mend` + `plot_beat`.
- **Continuant · govern** (high ward) — `feature_key` tag `govern` + `plot_beat`.
- **Continuant · grow** (mild ward) — `feature_key` tag `grow` + `plot_beat`.
- **Drift · learn** (high ward) — `feature_key` tag `learn` + `plot_beat`.
- **Drift · play** (mild ward) — `feature_key` tag `play` + `plot_beat`.
