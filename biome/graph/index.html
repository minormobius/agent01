biome · the trophic web — one force-directed web, land + lake, sized by living biomass

Every node is a real organism wearing its own photograph; its area is its present standing biomass in the closed run. The terrestrial food web grows on the cylinder floor; the aquatic web lives in the lake basin. They are trophically disjoint — no land animal eats a lake plankter — yet abiotically fused: both breathe one air, draw one nitrogen pool, drain to one detritus pool, and feed one crew from one larder. Those shared pools, drawn down the shoreline, are the interface. Drag nodes, scroll to zoom, hover for a card.  → global box model · lake bioengine · build your own
