biome · fountain & sun — looking down the axis (2-D cross-section)

Module 2 resolved the cylinder in radius; this is the other free dimension — azimuth. We're looking straight down the barrel: the linear sun at the axis (centre), the canopy on the rim (edge), and a fountain at the low point ("Fond du Lac") throwing quasi-treated pond water inward. At 760× Earth's spin the Coriolis force is comparable to gravity, so the jet doesn't go straight up — it curves into a sheet, aerating the water and lofting surface air through the inversion in one act. The jet (Fond du Lac)

Aim straight in (0°) for max reach; the spin still curves it. The fan clears the inversion and spreads water; mist aerates best but stalls low; the jet punches deepest as a column. Coriolis lays it all down prograde — that azimuthal smear is your irrigation.

The axial sun — luminous flux

The sun is a line, so irradiance falls as 1/r — to flood the rim you pour a colossal line power down the axis, and nearly all of it lands as heat the shell must radiate. The bare food need is tiny by comparison: you light for area and living space, not for calories.
