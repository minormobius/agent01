The berg is a strange attractor you can play with. Its body is tens of thousands of iterations of a de Jong map x'=sin(a·y)−cos(b·x); y'=sin(c·x)−cos(d·y) warped into an iceberg. Drag a site to move it · drag the open water to morph the attractor's a/c · double-click the water to re-roll the whole map.

Depth = how much work the endpoint carries, log scale: deepest-worked near the sunlit tip, lightly-touched toward the benthic keel. Preferred metric is page views (Cloudflare, when the token has Zone Analytics: Read).

Commit depth (current metric) counts every endpoint — sub-paths included — across all refs, not just HEAD. Most work happens on claude/* feature branches and is squash-merged down to a few main-line commits, so HEAD alone hides the bulk: this repo has ~490 commits on HEAD but ~1,580 across all branches. Hover shows the HEAD count, the work that lived off-main, and total line churn.
