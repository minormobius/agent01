A field of soil, and a finger to poke it

Choose a soil on the texture triangle; a field of exactly that composition stretches out. Poke it and a true, mass-conserving dent forms — the soil you displace heaps into the rim, then settles the way that soil settles: sand slumps to its angle of repose, clay holds a steep little crater.

What you're looking at

The field is a height-field of soil. A poke presses a dish into it; the soil that was there has to go somewhere, so it's heaved up into the rim (mass is conserved). Then the surface relaxes: wherever the slope is steeper than that soil can stand, grains topple downhill until it settles. Dry sand can only hold ≈33°, so its craters slump wide and shallow; clay's cohesion lets it stand near-vertical, so its dents keep their walls. Move the point on the triangle and the same ground will behave like a different earth.
