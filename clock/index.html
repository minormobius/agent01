Helix Calendar

A supercoiled visualization of time. Two nested helices encode years, months, days, and hours into a single continuous spiral.

Topology

Macro helix — One full revolution per year. Each month occupies 1/12 of the circumference when viewed on-axis. The helix advances vertically with each revolution, so consecutive years stack.

Micro helix — Each day is one tight coil wound around the macro path. Within each month's arc you'll see ~30 individual day-loops. Hours map to position along each daily coil.

The result is a coiled coil: zoom out to read months and years, zoom in to see individual days spiraling past.

Reading the time

Brightness encodes time of day — midnight is dimmest, noon is brightest, with dawn/dusk transitions between.

White ring marks the current moment (today's date and hour).

Controls

Drag to orbit the camera around the helix.

Scroll to zoom in and out.

The view auto-rotates gently until you first interact.

Technical

Rendered with WebGPU. No frameworks, no build step, no backend. The geometry is a tube mesh extruded along the supercoil path with Phong shading, specular highlights, and rim lighting.

~1825 day-coils × 13 samples × 6-sided tube cross-section. All generated at load time on the client.
