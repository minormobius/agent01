Helix Calendar

A supercoiled visualization of time. Two nested helices encode years, months, days, and hours into a single continuous spiral.

Topology

Level 1 — Macro helix — One full revolution per year. Each month occupies 1/12 of the circumference. Years stack vertically.

Level 2 — Day coils — Each day is one tight coil wound around the macro path. ~30 day-loops per month. Brightness encodes hour of day.

Level 3 — Minute coils — Zoom all the way in to see 60 minute-coils spiraling around the current hour.

The result is a triple-nested coiled coil: zoom out for years, in for days, all the way in for minutes. A pulsing gold ring marks now.

Reading the time

Brightness encodes time of day — midnight is dimmest, noon is brightest, with dawn/dusk transitions between.

Gold ring pulses at the current moment — tracks your position on the helix in real time.

Controls

Drag to orbit the camera around the helix.

Scroll to zoom in and out.

The view auto-rotates gently until you first interact.

Technical

Rendered with WebGPU. No frameworks, no build step, no backend. The geometry is a tube mesh extruded along the supercoil path with Phong shading, specular highlights, and rim lighting.

Three LOD levels with smooth crossfade. L1 and L2 generated at load; L3 (minute detail) generated on-demand for the current hour. A pulsing now-marker ring regenerates each frame (~48 tris).
