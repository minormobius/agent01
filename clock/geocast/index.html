The rite

A geomancer works without counting: stab a quick line of dots in the earth, then another, and another. Each line is later tallied odd or even — odd leaves a single mark, even a pair — and four lines stack into one figure. Sixteen lines give the four Mothers; from them the whole shield of fifteen figures unfolds. Here are the raw marks; the reading is the next step.

Tradition uses sixteen lines for the four Mothers — the slider goes there too. If fifteen feels crowded, that's worth knowing before we build the tally on top.
