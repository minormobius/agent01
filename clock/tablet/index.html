What this is

The only known medieval geomantic machine: a copper-alloy plate inlaid with gold and silver, made by al-Mawṣilī in 1241/42. The diviner turned dials whose windows revealed configurations of silver points — a randomiser in brass, the uncounted hand. The four Mothers cast here raise the same shield the sand table does; the reading is composed from Robert Fludd (1704). New to the art? Read the manual →
