⛳ O'Neill Links

You're playing golf on the inside of an O'Neill cylinder. There's no real gravity — the hull spins, so "down" is centrifugal (ω²r, outward) and the floor curves up and over your head. Every shot also feels the Coriolis force (−2Ω×v), which bends the ball sideways in the air and keeps breaking it as it rolls.

The gold dotted line is where you're aiming — straight. Watch the ball's cyan trail peel away from it: that's the Coriolis bend. You have to aim off the pin to hole out. Press G to replay the exact shot under plain Earth gravity (the control) and feel the difference.
