🦆 spin-gravity duck

You're flying the same duck under two reference frames. The aerodynamics never change — only the body force does:

  • Earth — a uniform 1 g pulling straight down.
  • O'Neill cylinder — you sit in the hull's co-rotating frame, so the landscape curves up and over your head. "Gravity" is centrifugal (ω²r, outward) and weakens as you climb toward the axis; every motion also feels the Coriolis force (−2Ω×v). Fly spinward and you get heavier; fly toward the axis and you're deflected.

The default cylinder is hoop — the 8 km / 0.8 g hull from the hoop O'Neill model (ω ≈ 0.031 rad/s). Press C to cycle to tighter rings where Coriolis is wild.

The course. A procedurally generated run of 12 gates winds through each world — over the ground on Earth, spiralling down the curved interior in the cylinder. Fly through the gold (next) gate, follow the bearing in the HUD, clear them all, then set down gently on the landing pad — touchdowns are graded on descent rate and how level you are. N rolls a new course.

Touch / mouse: tap to flap (a wingbeat — forward + up), and where you tap relative to the centre steers — above centre pitches the nose up, the sides bank you into a turn. Hold to keep flapping and climb.
