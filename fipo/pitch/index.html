FIPO — an archive of pitches for bad science-fiction films that were never made. Bad cinema is not noise: it is a small, structured region of movie-space — the space of pitches somebody thought would sell. Every seed addresses one specimen, deterministically, forever. ← → walk the archive; r jumps.

    
This is the archive in three movements: the genome (above — every field of the pitch, derived), the projection (the one-sheet a producer would have made of it: the painting prompt assembled from genome-traced slots, the type pass composited deterministically — renders run in Actions), and the judge (pairwise, genome-in-context: coherence and sincerity, keeping the model in the second-tier region where incompetence is trained, never winked at).
