FIPO — an archive of pitches for bad science-fiction films that were never made. Bad cinema is not noise: it is a small, structured region of movie-space — the space of pitches somebody thought would sell. Every seed addresses one specimen, deterministically, forever. ← → walk the archive; r jumps.

    
This is phase one of three: the genome (here), then the judge (what makes one specimen intriguingly bad and another merely dull), then the projection (the poster a producer would have made of it). The genome leads because the title is derived, never authored — and because the failure modes must cohere before they can be scored.
