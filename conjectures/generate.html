the conjecture machine

procedurally generated open problems · with a solvability oracle

Feed it a seed; it hands back a conjecture — a plausible-sounding open problem assembled from a grammar of objects, quantifiers, and claims. Then the solvability oracle reads the statement's shape — its domain, its quantifier structure, how a counterexample would look — and estimates the odds it would still be unproven in 2126, using the same difficulty rubric the real index applies to the 193 genuine conjectures. It shows its work.

This is a Borges machine, so it is deterministic: a seed always yields the same conjecture, and the URL is a permalink. It is also, emphatically, fiction — most generated statements are false, trivial, or ill-posed (that is the joke: a Library of Babel of conjectures). The oracle judges the form, not the truth; it cannot prove anything. Names are invented, and any resemblance to real mathematicians is coincidental.
