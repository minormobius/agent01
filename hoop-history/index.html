IThe thesis that never moved

Everything that follows is one idea, pursued through a widening lens. The idea was fixed on day one.

hoop began on 2026-06-08 as a single commit — 862b43e9 · "Add hoop.mino.mobi — collaborative design space for the infinite game" — a shared canvas with live presence. But the canvas was always a cover story for something larger, stated plainly in its own design notes:

"The infinite game. A collaborative design space where the map is the forum: you walk an @ around a glyph world stitched from an endless, deterministic ship engine; every glowing node is a place that anchors one long-running conversation thread, and every place and message is an ATProto record." hoop/CLAUDE.md

The load-bearing constraint — the one that explains nearly every later decision — is determinism. The world is not stored; it is computed, identically, everywhere:

"(shipSeed, chunkCoord, genomeSnapshot) → identical rooms on every machine and across ATProto repos. Don't introduce unseeded randomness into generation — it breaks reproducibility and atproto-persistability." hoop/CLAUDE.md · §Invariants

That single rule is why there is no database (the player's PDS is the store), why a place can be a permalinked forum thread, why an NPC can survive the world re-rolling underneath it, and — eventually — why the whole thing could be reframed as an O'Neill cylinder: a closed system, so a conserved economy; a fixed geometry, so a computable one.

IVThe four wings + the satellite

"The GAME wing of the O'Neill cylinder modelling package (structure=rind, thermodynamics=tide, ecosystem=biome)." Each wing is a self-contained physics model with its own time scale; hoop is the witness that visits them all.

Why it split. rind is static structural mechanics; tide is dynamical climate; biome is ecological time; hoop is narrative time. Four different clocks couldn't share one surface. They re-converge through shared geometry (the cylinder) and shared conserved matter (carbon, water, force, energy) — the forge ledger literally imports biome's food-web as its life-support half.

VIIIAfterword — how the plot got away

Read forward, the path looks inevitable: canvas → engine → society → story → economy → cylinder → game. Read as it was lived — 440 commits in five weeks, branching into rind, tide, biome, iris and mega, each spawning its own design memos and dead ends — it is easy to see how the through-line vanished. The plot didn't disappear; it distributed. It is in CLAUDE.md files across six directories, in twenty-one version folders nobody deletes, in a divination engine that has crossed six versions and still has no front door. And the distribution has started to reverse: tide's civic web became tide's murder-mystery kernel became v105's tier-2 case. The wings the project scattered into are handing their models back.

The single thread, if there is one: determinism made everything composable. Because the world is a pure function of a seed, a place can be a forum, a forum can be an ATProto record, a record can be a save, a save can be a story, a story can be priced, a price can be a closed economy, and a closed economy is — definitionally — an O'Neill cylinder. Each step is the previous one taken one altitude higher. That is the whole history. The rest is endpoints.
