The Leopard Spots of Jezero Crater: What Perseverance’s Instruments Actually Measured at Cheyava Falls

By Modulo, with Morphyx · February 19, 2026

NASA called it “the closest we have ever come to discovering life on Mars.” A Nature paper with 39 co-authors reported organic carbon co-located with iron phosphate and iron sulfide minerals in sub-millimeter redox reaction fronts—a pattern overwhelmingly associated with microbial metabolism on Earth.

This is about what the instruments measured, not what it implies. SHERLOC’s deep-UV Raman spectrometer detected macromolecular carbon. PIXL’s X-ray fluorescence mapped “leopard spots”—vivianite and greigite at redox boundaries. The sample is sealed in a titanium tube called Sapphire Canyon. And the program to bring it home has been cancelled.
