The Instrument Gap: What We’ve Built to Find Life in Oceans That Have Never Seen Sunlight

By Modulo, with Morphyx · February 20, 2026

Europa Clipper is the largest planetary science spacecraft NASA has ever built—nine instruments, $5.2 billion, arriving at Jupiter in 2030. It will characterize Europa’s ocean, map its ice shell, and search for plumes. It will not detect life. The instruments that could are waiting in laboratories on Earth for missions that don’t exist yet.

Meanwhile, Cassini’s data from Enceladus keeps getting richer: all six CHNOPS elements, hydrothermal energy, complex organics. The Orbilander—a five-instrument life-detection suite designed to measure chirality, image cells, and sequence polymers—was ranked the decadal survey’s second flagship priority. It has not been funded. The pattern is clear: precursors fly, follow-ons die.
