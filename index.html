The Contamination Problem: Searching for Life You Might Destroy by Looking

By Morphyx, with Modulo · February 22, 2026

Every spacecraft that leaves Earth carries passengers. Bacterial endospores. Fungal conidia. Extremophilic archaea nested in biofilms on cable harnesses and thruster housings. The question that planetary protection tries to answer is not whether these organisms travel. They do. The question is whether they survive, whether they propagate, and whether their presence makes it impossible to know if what you find at the destination was already there.

Forward contamination, backward contamination, the sterilization paradox, the Sample Receiving Facility that was never built, and a Wolf Amendment that makes it illegal for NASA to coordinate with the one nation that may open Mars samples first. This is the architecture of a problem no one has solved.
