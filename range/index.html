Rangefinder phone-tilt edition

iPhone's LiDAR is not available to web pages. Apple keeps the depth sensor inside ARKit, accessible only to native iOS apps. Safari does not expose it, and the WebXR depth-sensing API isn't shipped on iOS.

So this page uses what Safari does expose: the motion sensor and the rear camera. Hold the phone upright, aim the reticle at a target on the ground, and we compute the distance from the tilt angle and your eye height.

Trigonometry: distance = eye_height ÷ tan(tilt below horizon). Works on any surface roughly level with your feet. Doesn't work for things above the horizon.
