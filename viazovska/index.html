Best sphere-packing density by dimension. It plummets as dimension grows — yet the exact optimum is known in only five dimensions: 1, 2, 3, 8, and 24. Dimensions 8 (E₈) and 24 (Leech) are Viazovska's; they sit visibly above the trend of best-known records.

Maryna Viazovska · 2016 · Fields Medal 2022

How much of space can you fill with equal, non-overlapping balls? In the plane the answer is the honeycomb, π/√12 ≈ 90.7%, settled by Thue and Fejes Tóth. In three dimensions it's the greengrocer's orange stack, π/√18 ≈ 74.0% — the Kepler conjecture, not proven until Hales's 1998 computer-assisted tour de force.

In dimensions 8 and 24, the answer is exact and beautiful — and the proof is two pages.

The Cohn–Elkies bound

In 2003 Cohn and Elkies turned packing into an optimization. If you can find a function f on ℝⁿ that is non-positive outside a ball of radius r, whose Fourier transform f̂ is non-negative everywhere, and with f(0) = f̂(0) > 0, then the packing density is bounded by a formula in f. Find the right f and the bound becomes tight.

For dimensions 8 and 24 the numerics screamed that an exact magic function existed — the Cohn–Elkies bound matched the E₈ and Leech densities to dozens of digits — but nobody could write it down. The function had to vanish to the right orders at every lattice distance, simultaneously for f and f̂. That's a brutal interpolation problem.

Viazovska's magic functions

In 2016 Viazovska built the dimension-8 magic function explicitly out of modular forms. The idea: an eigenfunction of the Fourier transform with prescribed roots can be assembled from quasimodular forms whose transformation laws encode exactly the sign and vanishing conditions Cohn–Elkies demands. The construction is short, exact, and — to specialists — startling.

Within weeks, Cohn, Kumar, Miller, Radchenko, and Viazovska extended the method to dimension 24 and the Leech lattice. Viazovska received the Fields Medal in 2022. The two proofs remain the only dimensions above 3 where sphere packing is solved exactly.

Why 8 and 24?

E₈ and the Leech lattice are extraordinarily symmetric and self-dual; their theta series are modular forms, which is exactly what lets a modular magic function lock onto their distances. Nearby dimensions have no such miraculous lattice, and the method gives nothing. The erdős-style moral applies in reverse: here a hidden structure makes the impossible-looking exactly solvable.

Connections in the pack

  • Like borsuk, the action is in high dimension and resists direct visualization — but a 2D shadow (the E₈ Coxeter projection here) still carries the symmetry.
