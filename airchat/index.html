OAuth keeps your password on Bluesky's side — you get redirected there to approve airchat, then back. App-password is the fallback if OAuth isn't supported by your PDS. Either way, the session token never reaches the browser; we store it server-side and you get an opaque cookie.

Voice posts go to your PDS as com.minomobi.airchat.voice records. Audio is a blob on your repo; the transcript is a string on the record. Reads are public to anyone; writes go through us so you can use Whisper without exposing the key.

Closed alpha — writes are gated to a small whitelist. Reading the feed works for everyone.
