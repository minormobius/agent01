dihedral (2,2,n) · 48 cells

(2,2,n) — the infinite family, one per integer
A spherical kaleidoscope is a Schwarz triangle (p,q,r) with 1/p+1/q+1/r > 1. The complete list: the (2,2,n) family (any n → 4n cells) plus exactly three exceptions — tetra (2,3,3), octa (2,3,4), icosa (2,3,5). Subdivide splits each triangle on a geodesic grid: arbitrary density, symmetry intact.
