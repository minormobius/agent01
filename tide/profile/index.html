Gravity is pure kinematics of the spin: g(r)=ω²r, zero on the axis, full at the rim. Nothing the air does changes it.
Pressure comes from hydrostatic balance in the rotating frame — effective gravity points outward, so dP/dr = ρ·ω²r. For an isothermal ideal gas this integrates to P(r) = P_axis·exp(S·(r/R)²) with S = Mω²R²/(2RT) = v²_rim/(2c²); the rim/axis ratio is exactly e^S.
"Non-spinning pressure" P₀ fixes the amount of air. Same air, at rest conserves total moles — the area-weighted mean stays P₀, the axis drops and the rim rises. Held on axis instead pins the centreline at P₀. Isothermal, single-gas; the full GCM-style column (sun, fog, convection, the centrifugal adiabat) lives in atmosphere.
