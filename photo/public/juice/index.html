How much of this can we actually model?

The honest answer, idea by idea. The lab above is the real thing running, not a video.

Fully modelled

Refractive-index slider

This is exact. Every pixel is a ray refracted through the glass with Snell's law; the slider is the index n (1.0 air → 2.42 diamond). Watch the background squeeze and flip as n climbs, and the Fresnel edge-glow harden. Real physics, 60 fps.

Fully modelled

3D cubes & total internal reflection

Switch the shape to a cube and push n up. Past the critical angle the ray can't escape a face and reflects inside the solid — genuine TIR, traced for several bounces, the same effect that traps light in a diamond or an optical fibre. TIR'd rays are tinted so you can see them ricochet.

Mostly modelled

Birefringence mode

We trace two rays at once — an ordinary and extraordinary index — so the background doubles, exactly like text seen through calcite. The polariser knob fades between them via Malus's law. What we don't do is full crystal-axis tensor optics; it's the visible result, not the stress-engineering tool.

Concept + real beam

Laser-pointer homescreen widget

A web page can't be an iOS home-screen widget — that's an Apple SDK surface. But we can model what it'd show: a live laser raytraced through a glass drop, bending and total-internal-reflecting as you aim. Drag the dot. The widget chrome is a mock; the optics inside it are real.
