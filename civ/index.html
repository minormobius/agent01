civ — the world engine suite

One deterministic stack from planet to person, spread across four sites and unified here. mappa generates the world; civ runs ten millennia of emergent history on it; polis grows the cities; names and org mint the people, cultures and institutions that populate all of it. Every layer is a pure function of a seed — a URL is a world, a run, a city, a company.

A dependency-free procedural planet: tectonic plates → elevation → climate → Whittaker biomes → rivers. The same integer seed yields the same world in node, browser, or Worker — everything downstream imports mappa/engine.js rather than forking it.

Deep-time climate forcing, an interestingness battery scoring each world, and ?w= permalinks that are the world config.

II The history — this site

civ
10 000 yr · civilizations
live · API

A coevolutionary agent-based civilization simulation. Individuals turn over by birth and death; lineages, cultures and institutions carry identity across ten millennia. Nothing about eras is scripted — agriculture and industry are emergent phase transitions.

One run is a pure function of (world, config, civSeed, ticks): the same URL regenerates the same chronicle, bit-identical at the edge and in your browser, and edge-caches forever.

III The city

polis ↗

expansion — in progress

City-economics on the mappa cell graph: mappa answers where a city sits; polis grows nucleus → town → city → metropolis from a seed, with the economy as a forcing on one graph-Laplacian field.

Currently theory-heavy with a working vertical slice (site scoring, founding engine, Physarum trade arteries, whole-history chronicle). It already imports the real mappa engine — the missing seam is upward, into civ.

The unification roadmap

The suite grew as separate tendrils; the full plan for pulling them into one stack is in STRATEGY.md. The short version:

  1. I — one address. This hub; the dashboard moved to /dash/ (old run permalinks redirect); every layer keeps its seed-is-the-URL contract.
  2. II — one naming voice (shipped). civ's cultures, people, beliefs, institutions and cities are named by the rite/names engine, one culture-pack blend per civ culture; names:'legacy' in the config replays the old strings. Chronicle hashes are unchanged — names were never part of the hash.
  3. III — the civ → polis handoff (shipped). /api/civ/sites emits every state's rise as a founding (seat lon/lat, tick, toponym, siteSeed); polis grows the city on the same world at the same cell, seeded by the siteSeed string. The dashboard links each founding straight into polis.
  4. IV — institutions with insides. civ's guilds, temples and states become org-engine organisations via siteSeed; notable people in chronicles become full org persons (and valid hoop NPCs).
  5. V — outward. Frozen chronicles as ATProto records, the fable /city wing, and hoop as the walkable end of the same stack.

Everything here is deterministic and permalink-stable: if you can link it, you can regenerate it, forever. · mino.mobi
