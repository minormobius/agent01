Math uab Mathonwy

The Fourth and last Branch of the Mabinogi — the branch of transformation. Math, the old magician-king of Gwynedd, can only live with his feet in a maiden's lap save in time of war; and his nephew Gwydion, the great trickster-enchanter, starts a war on purpose — stealing Pryderi's Otherworld swine with illusory horses, and killing Pryderi himself — so that his brother can wrong the king's foot-holder. From that outrage runs the whole branch: the brothers punished as paired beasts; a child denied a name, arms, and a wife by his own mother's curses; a wife conjured out of flowers; her betrayal and the murder of Lleu; his flight as a wounded eagle; and a reckoning worked, like everything here, by the wand. It ends where the Four Branches end — with the Pryderi thread closed and a new lord over Gwynedd.

The Middle Welsh source is the Welsh Wikisource reading text (CC BY-SA 4.0) from the White Book / Red Book tradition, the same edition that gives Pwyll on this site; the original English translation runs alongside it movement by movement, with character cards, character web, story graph, motif index, mythograph and a faithful storybook retelling — the same apparatus built for Culhwch ac Olwen, Pwyll Pendefig Dyfed, Branwen ferch Llŷr, Manawydan uab Llŷr, Owain, Sir Orfeo, and Sir Gawain and the Green Knight.
