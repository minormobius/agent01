Branwen ferch Llŷr

The Second Branch of the Mabinogi — the great Welsh tragedy. A marriage made to bind two islands; an insult that cannot be unmade; a cauldron that gives the dead back to battle but not to speech; and the giant king Bendigeidfran, whose severed head feasts his seven survivors for fourscore years. It begins in alliance and ends with two islands laid waste.

A deep read of Branwen ferch Llŷr, the Second Branch of the Mabinogi — the sister-tale of Pwyll Pendefig Dyfed (the First Branch) and, with it, a foundation of the Welsh prose tradition. The Middle Welsh source is the public-domain edition of Rhys & Evans (Oxford, 1887) from the Red Book of Hergest; the original English translation runs alongside it movement by movement, with character cards, character web, story graph, motif index, mythograph and a faithful storybook retelling — the same apparatus built for Culhwch ac Olwen, Pwyll Pendefig Dyfed, Owain, Sir Orfeo, and Sir Gawain and the Green Knight. This site sits alongside the broader Arthurian and Brittonic historiography at read.mino.mobi/pendragon, where the cross-tale comparative layer (motifs, Propp functions, character archetypes across all the annotated tales) lives.
