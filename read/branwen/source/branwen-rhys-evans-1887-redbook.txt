Branwen uerch Lyr — the Second Branch of the Mabinogi (Middle Welsh, Red Book of Hergest)
Source: "The Text of the Mabinogion and other Welsh Tales from the Red Book
   of Hergest," ed. John Rhys & J. Gwenogvryn Evans (Oxford, 1887),
   running head "Branwen, daughter of Llyr" (pp. 26 ff.). Public domain.
   Internet Archive: archive.org/details/textofmabinogion00rhysiala
   The same volume supplies the Pwyll, Culhwch and Owain texts on this site.
   Underlying manuscripts: the White Book of Rhydderch (c. 1350) and the
   Red Book of Hergest (c. 1382-1410).
License: Public domain (edition pre-1900; OCR text via Internet Archive).

NOTE ON THIS FILE — RAW OCR, TO BE NORMALISED:
   This is the raw OCR of the 1887 edition's special Welsh typeface, which the
   OCR mis-reads systematically (medieval w/u as "O"/"6", r as "z"/"x",
   long-s as "f", illuminated capitals as "J[", "J)", etc.). It is preserved
   here as the diplomatic anchor, NOT as a reading text; the Welsh shown in
   tale.js is normalised by hand from this base, movement by movement (the
   workflow used for Pwyll and Owain). The branch opens "Llyma yr eil geinc
   o'r Mabinogi" / "Bendigeidfran fab Llyr a oedd frenhin coronawc ar yr ynys
   honn" and closes "Ac uelly y teruyna y geinc honn yma o'r Mabinogi, o
   achaws paluawt Branwen."
