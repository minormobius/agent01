Manawydan uab Llŷr

The Third Branch of the Mabinogi — the sequel to the tragedy. Two of Branwen's seven survivors come home to a marriage and a feast; then a mist falls on the gorsedd of Arberth and lifts on an empty world — no house, no beast, no smoke, no neighbour, only the four of them left in a wasteland. Driven to England to live by their hands, then back to a Dyfed that swallows Pryderi and Rhiannon whole, Manawydan alone keeps his head — and lifts the whole enchantment with a pregnant mouse, a tiny gallows, and a bargain held to the last word. The wasteland branch, and the only branch where wit, not war, wins.

A deep read of Manawydan uab Llŷr, the Third Branch of the Mabinogi — the direct sequel to Branwen ferch Llŷr (the Second Branch) and, with Pwyll Pendefig Dyfed (the First), the heart of the Four Branches. The Middle Welsh source is the Welsh Wikisource reading text (CC BY-SA 4.0) from the White Book / Red Book tradition, the same edition that gives Pwyll on this site; the original English translation runs alongside it movement by movement, with character cards, character web, story graph, motif index, mythograph and a faithful storybook retelling — the same apparatus built for Culhwch ac Olwen, Pwyll Pendefig Dyfed, Branwen ferch Llŷr, Owain, Sir Orfeo, and Sir Gawain and the Green Knight. This site sits alongside the broader Arthurian and Brittonic historiography at read.mino.mobi/pendragon, where the cross-tale comparative layer (motifs, Propp functions, character archetypes across all the annotated tales) lives.
