Owain, neu Iarlles y Ffynnon

The Lady of the Fountain — one of the Three Welsh Romances, the Brittonic twin of Chrétien de Troyes' Yvain: a knight of Arthur's court rides to the magic fountain, defeats its Black Knight, weds the widowed Lady he has made — then forgets her, runs mad in the wilderness, and is redeemed with the help of a grateful lion.

A deep read of Owain, or the Lady of the Fountain (Welsh: Owein, neu Chwedyl Iarlles y Ffynnawn), one of the Three Welsh Romances. The Middle Welsh source is the public-domain edition of Rhys & Evans (Oxford, 1887) from the Red Book of Hergest — the same volume that supplies Culhwch ac Olwen on this site; the original English translation runs alongside it movement by movement, with character cards, character web, story graph, motif index, mythograph and a faithful middle-grade storybook retelling — the same apparatus built for Sir Gawain and the Green Knight, Culhwch ac Olwen, Sir Orfeo, and Pwyll Pendefig Dyfed. This site sits alongside the broader Arthurian and Brittonic historiography at read.mino.mobi/pendragon, where the cross-tale comparative layer (motifs, Propp functions, character archetypes across all the annotated tales) lives — and where Owain's kinship with Chrétien de Troyes' Yvain anchors the Welsh↔Continental branch of the evolutionary tree.
