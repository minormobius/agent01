Owein, neu Chwedyl Iarlles y Ffynnawn — Middle Welsh text (Red Book of Hergest)
Source: "The Text of the Mabinogion and other Welsh Tales from the Red Book
   of Hergest," ed. John Rhys & J. Gwenogvryn Evans (Oxford, 1887),
   pp. 163-192 (running head "Owein and Lunet"). Public domain.
   Internet Archive: archive.org/details/textofmabinogion00rhysiala
   The same volume supplies the Culhwch ac Olwen text on this site, and is
   the public-domain comparison cited for Pwyll. Underlying manuscript: the
   Red Book of Hergest (Llyfr Coch Hergest), Jesus College Oxford MS 111,
   c. 1382-1410; the tale is also preserved in the White Book of Rhydderch
   (Llyfr Gwyn Rhydderch), c. 1350.
License: Public domain (edition pre-1900; OCR text via Internet Archive).

NOTE ON THIS FILE — RAW OCR, TO BE NORMALISED:
   This is the raw OCR of the 1887 edition, which sets the tale in a special
   Welsh typeface that the OCR mis-reads systematically: medieval w/u renders
   as capital "O" or "6", r often as "z" or "x", long-s as "f", and the
   illuminated capitals as "J[", "J)", "9", "3", "°|f" etc. It is preserved
   here verbatim as the diplomatic anchor, NOT as a reading text. The Welsh
   shown in tale.js is normalised from this base movement by movement (the
   same workflow used for Culhwch ac Olwen, whose tale.js carries a normalised
   reading text rather than this raw transcription), cross-checked against the
   White Book where the OCR is unrecoverable. The tale opens at p.163
   ("Yr amherawdyr Arthur a oedd yng Nghaer Llion ar Wysg") and closes at
   p.192 ("Ar chwedyl hwn a elwir chwedyl Iarlles y Ffynnawn").
