The old way (serial)

Requirements → Architecture → BOM → Schedule → Test. Each gate hands to the next. Information flows one way. A surprise late in test rewinds weeks of work to architecture.

Diffusion

All facets exist at once, each at some level of resolution. Every iteration refines every facet using every other facet's current state. Constraints propagate the moment they appear.

What AI does

AI keeps the field coherent — perturb requirements and architecture, BOM, schedule update together. Late discoveries ripple and dampen instead of resetting. Faster cycles, fewer surprises, a design you can navigate as a whole.

Why this matters for the global process

Today's design hand-offs are lossy on purpose — each stage freezes its inputs so the next can plan against something stable. That stability has a cost: discoveries late in the chain mean tearing up something settled. The serial process is built around protecting upstream decisions.

Diffusion inverts it. Every facet is always in motion, just at different temperatures. Late information is cheap because nothing was frozen; it's just one more constraint added to the field. The team's job stops being "guard your stage" and starts being "shape the field's convergence."
