what you're looking at

The ring topology lifted into z. A weave is a weave because threads go over and under as they cross — so every crossing is a control point at ±amp, and the height between is a smoothstep ⇒ a zero-grade flat sits at every crossing.

• 6 white ops × 6 engines, counter-woven (K 6,6), each thread weaving over/under.
• assembly (inner) & reclaim (outer) rings, each weaving over/under around its loop, oppositely to the other.

the antechambers

That zero-grade flat is exactly where an antechamber belongs — the neutral z = 0 mid-plane where the two floors meet at grade (no ladder). — proposed — 36 white×engine (one per pair) + 12 beefy ring chambers: at low tightness a ring crossing and its neighbour merge, so one chamber junctions the ring + two threads. They're the flat squares.
